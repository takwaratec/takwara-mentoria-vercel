# Technical Module 03: Vegetable Polyurethane and Bio-Weld

**Mentorship Journey — Takwara Technology**

*The "Secret" behind high-performance connections and castor bean chemistry.*

---

## 1. The Heart of Takwara Technology

While traditional woodworking uses white glue, epoxy, or nails, bamboo bioconstruction requires an adhesive and structural sealant that follows the flexibility of organic fiber without drying it out or breaking it. The answer to this is **Vegetable Polyurethane (PU)** .

Vegetable PU is the basis of the **Bio-Weld Kit**. Unlike conventional polyurethanes (mineral, petroleum-derived, with highly volatile and toxic solvents), our resin is produced from castor bean oil (*Ricinus communis L.*).

## 2. History and High Performance (Social Proof)

The technology was commercially synthesized in Brazil in 1994, at the University of São Paulo (USP / São Carlos) by Prof. Dr. Gilberto Chierice.

Originally developed as an elastomer for insulating underground cables for Eletrobrás (far surpassing the wear of fossil-based resins), Prof. Chierice's research advanced into **biomedicine**.

Castor bean polyol creates a polymer with bone-like strength (used in human cranial prostheses at InCor and approved as a biomaterial of extremely high compatibility). In bamboo structures, the vegetable "weld" behaves exactly like a prosthesis: it fills voids (through nodular injection), inhibits fungi and bacteria (intrinsic property of castor bean), and unifies the fibers.

> **Tip**
> **Exclusive Technical Advantages of Vegetable PU:**
> 1. **Non-toxic:** Free of hazardous solvents (no methylene chloride or strong industrial acetone). Does not emit strong odors during curing.
> 2. **Biological Compatibility:** Being a vegetable oil, it hydrates bamboo fibers during polymerization.
> 3. **Absolute Waterproofing:** Resists weathering far superior to marine varnishes.
> 4. **Flame Retardant:** Does not propagate flames, qualifying it for structures in public spaces and warehouses.
> 5. **Biodegradable (very long term):** After its long service life, it degrades in the environment (~30-year cycle in advanced composting).

## 3. Proven Laboratory Performance (Itecons Tests)

Vegetable PU Imperveg (RQI 132 line) was subjected to performance tests by Itecons (Portugal) — the data below is extracted from reports LAB_001 to LAB_006 of the MQTF Scientific Archive:

| Test | Standard | Result | Significance |
|------|----------|--------|--------------|
| **Adhesion (Pull-Off)** | NP EN 1504-2:2006 | **1.8 MPa** (min. 1.0 MPa) | The concrete broke before the resin |
| **Adhesion after 70°C** | Thermal aging | **1.2 MPa** | No vitrification or loss of flexibility |
| **Thermal Shock (60 cycles)** | EN 13687 | **1.1 MPa** | No bubbles, cracks, or delamination |
| CO₂ Barrier (Sd) | NP EN 1062-6 | **365 meters** | 0.67 mm film = 365 m of static air |
| **Vapor Barrier** | ISO 7783:2018 | **0.37 g/m².d** | 99.85% reduction in water transmission |
| **Chemical Resistance** | HCl 10% / NaOH 20% / H₂S | **Stable (28 days)** | Total inertia, no swelling |

*Reference: LAB_001 to LAB_006 — Itecons Tests. MQTF Archive / BNDES Dossier 04_REGENCIA_CIENTIFICA/03_ENSAIOS_LABORATORIO.*

### Why this matters in practice

- **1.8 MPa** adhesion means Bio-Weld anchors molecularly into bamboo — it is not a surface coating.
- **365 m Sd** means the resin shields the fiber against carbonation and moisture for decades.
- **Stable in HCl and NaOH** means it withstands sanitation and agro-industrial effluent environments.

## 4. Basic Handling (Pot Life and Application)

In the mentorship, students learn to handle the two components of Bio-Weld (Polyol and Isocyanate Pre-Polymer - MDI).

- **Mixing (Ratio):** The exact proportion (typically 1:1 by volume or as per manufacturer specification) ensures the polycondensation reaction occurs safely.
- **Pot Life:** The exothermic reaction requires planning. Working time (before curing hardens the mixture in the pot) depends on ambient temperature — approximately 30–40 min at 25°C. The material heats up during catalysis.
- **Ideal Application Temperature:** 18°C to 35°C. Below this, curing is very slow; above this, pot life is drastically reduced.
- **Nodular Injection:** The technique of injecting the mixture into the connections between culms (the "nodes") ensures the formation of a structural monolith: an indestructible chassis.

> 📊 **Practical application:** For a typical joint of two 3" bamboo culms, use ~50 ml of the mixture (25 ml of each component). Full curing for mechanical strength occurs within 24–48 h. The exothermic peak reaches ~50–60°C and is safe for the operator with basic PPE (nitrile gloves and goggles).
