# Research Dossier: Comparative Scientific Analysis of Biopolymers and Waterproofing Agents

**Status:** Consolidated (Scientific Review V2 — Full Sweep)
**Data Source:** Scientific Archive of Mulheres Que Tecem a Floresta (MQTF) and Itecons Tests (Portugal).

## 1. Research Objective

This dossier aims to establish the matrix of technical, chemical, and environmental superiority of **Vegetable Polyurethane (Castor PU — e.g., Imperveg RQI 132)** in relation to petrochemical resins (Epoxy, Conventional PUR) and so-called ecological systems (Latex and "Water-Based" Acrylics). The foundation of the arguments rests on an extensive battery of laboratory tests (`LAB_001` to `LAB_006`), medical and toxicological literature (`SCI_025`, `SCI_028`, `SCI_030`), and official documentation (MSDS).

---

## 2. Evaluation Methodology: The 6-Pillar Ruler

To avoid the pitfalls of "Greenwashing," evaluating a high-performance polymer for bioconstruction requires 6 non-negotiable axes:

1. **Adhesion (Pull-Off) and Cohesion:** Ability to anchor molecularly to the substrate.
2. **Extreme Durability and Thermal Shock:** Survival of the thermal expansion of vegetable fiber without cracking.
3. **Chemical Resistance:** Immunity to hydrolysis attacks, biogenesis (H₂S), and aggressive acids.
4. **Gas and Vapor Tightness:** Real measurable seal.
5. **Toxicity and Biocompatibility:** Safety in application and potability after curing.
6. **Carbon Footprint and Life Cycle:** Renewable primary source matrix.

---

## 3. Competitor Analysis: The Fall of Myths

### A. Acrylic Resins and "Water-Based Liquid Membranes"

Sold under strong ecological appeal for not containing aggressive solvents, they fatally fail under the light of engineering:

- **Solids Content and Shrinkage:** Due to water evaporation, they lose volume and generate severe shrinkage, stressing microcracks.
- **Hydrolysis Vulnerability:** The acrylic matrix does not withstand continuous ponding. Over months, water degrades the molecular chain, softening the resin and causing chronic infiltration.
- **Inefficient Gas Barrier:** High permeability to CO₂, allowing carbonation of concrete structures beneath the coating.

### B. Latex (Natural or Synthetic Rubber)

- **UV Degradation:** Double bonds in the polyisoprene chain are destroyed by ultraviolet radiation. The material "dries out," cracks, and crumbles.
- **Toxic Processing:** Despite being natural (in extraction), it requires sulfur-based curing/vulcanization or products that release toxic residues, severely limiting potability.

### C. Epoxy and PUR (Petrochemical Polyurethane)

The elite of high-traffic coatings has its dark counterpart:

- **Acute Toxicity (Free TDI/MDI):** The MSDS of commercial foams and petroleum-based polyurethanes reports *H351* (Suspected of causing cancer) and *H334* (Asthma and anaphylactic shock). Handling and curing release hazardous VOCs.
- **Mechanical Cracking:** Epoxy cures forming an excessively rigid solid. In joints between resin and natural fibers (e.g., bamboo), the difference in thermal expansion coefficients breaks the interface, causing delamination.

## 4. Empirical Evidence: Vegetable Polyurethane (The "Forest Adhesive")

Based on Itecons laboratory data and academic records approved in the MQTF Project, vegetable polyurethane derived from castor bean oil (*Ricinus communis*) obtained the following definitive reports:

### 4.1. Adhesive and Structural Resistance (The Minimum Standard is Crushed)

According to standard **NP EN 1504-2:2006**, a protective coating needs *1.0 MPa* of strength to be approved.

- **[LAB_001] Standard Pull-Off:** The resin reached **1.8 MPa**, with **cohesive rupture in concrete** (the concrete broke before the resin delaminated). This is extreme molecular anchoring.
- **[LAB_002] Thermal Aging (continuous 70°C):** Tension remained safe at **1.2 MPa**. No vitrification or loss of flexibility (elastomer stability).
- **[LAB_006] Thermal Shock (Freeze-Thaw + Chloride Salts):** Subjected to standard **EN 13687** (60 extreme cycles), the resin achieved **1.1 MPa**. There were no signs of blistering or delamination, proving absolute shielding against crystallized salts.

### 4.2. Protection Barriers and Chemical Inertia

- **[LAB_003] Severe Corrosion:** Tested for 28 days submerged in Hydrochloric Acid (10%), Sodium Hydroxide (20% — combating saponification), and saturated Seawater. Result: **Total Inertia**, no softening (*swelling*). It is immune to biogenic corrosion by hydrogen sulfide gas (H₂S), essential for Wastewater Treatment Plants (WWTPs).
- **[LAB_004] Carbonation Blocking (NP EN 1062-6):** A 0.67 mm film achieved an Equivalent Air performance (Sd) of **365 meters** and a resistance factor (μ) of **546,000**. It shields CO₂ impeccably.
- **[LAB_005] Vapor Barrier (ISO 7783:2018):** Reduction of water diffusion of the substrate from 252 g/m².d to minuscule **0.37 g/m².d**.

### 4.3. Biocompatibility and Human Health

- **Medical Non-toxicity:** In studies conducted by Vianna (USP, 1997) and Ignácio et al. (1996), it was proven through cross-reactive tests that Castor PU has no organic rejection or toxicity in the liver and spleen, being qualified for intra-osseous surgeries.
- **Potability Certification (MS Ordinance 888):** The complete absence of aggressive solvents and volatile components (such as those listed in the MSDS of petroleum-based competitors) qualifies it for direct and permanent contact with water intended for human consumption and sustainable irrigation.

---

## 5. Conclusive Technical Opinion and Commercial Direction

The laboratory metrics taken directly from the MQTF consortium scientifically prove that Castor PU (Imperveg System) **is not just a green alternative**. From a purely engineering standpoint, fossil-based polyurethane is a mechanically and toxicologically **inferior** material in scenarios of high thermal variability and direct human contact.

**Argument for the Architect / Builder (Mentorship):**

*"Acrylic resin meets the 'green seal' requirement, but condemns the project to hydrolytic maintenance within 2 years. Epoxy offers strength, but does not follow the natural expansion of living materials like bamboo and wood, cracking over time, besides being toxic to applicators. Vegetable PU offers 100% solids and 1.8 MPa of unbreakable anchoring, uniting the strength of concrete with organic thermal comfort."*
