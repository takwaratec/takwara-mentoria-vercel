# Technical Module 02: The Myth of Boron — Treatments and the Dark "Magic"

**Mentorship Journey — Takwara Technology**

*Why traditional bioconstruction fails at green chemistry.*

---

## 1. The Need for Preservation

Bamboo's Achilles' heel is starch. A mature culm attracts borers and fungi quickly. Historically, Asians mitigated this with immersion in running water for 28 days and smoking. However, on industrial scales and under the fast-paced capitalist model, the industry began injecting highly toxic poisons.

### The CCA and CCB Problem

To standardize inventory, the timber and bamboo industry worldwide adopted treatments based on heavy metals (Arsenic, Copper, Chromium). These water-soluble salts are introduced via autoclave. They generate chronic environmental liabilities in factories and poison workers. Developed countries have banned many of these substances, "outsourcing" the problem to liberal legislation in developing countries. For Takwara Technology, **the use of CCA/CCB is unacceptable and nullifies a project's "Sustainability" status.**

## 2. The Dark "Magic" of Boron (Borax) and the False Sense of Security

Faced with the toxicity of Arsenic, the bioconstruction market adopted **Borax (Sodium Borate)** as the organic "savior." Boron, although vital in ultra-trace concentrations, is being depleted worldwide and accumulating in groundwater tables.

> **Warning**
> A common treatment recipe teaches the use of 5% Borax diluted in water. This equates to contaminating a 500-liter tank with 25 kg of this powder, while agriculture recommends at most 3 kg per hectare. In the long term, this Borax bath pollutes the soil and groundwater, in addition to causing respiratory and reproductive problems with prolonged human contact.
>
> *Source: MQTF Collection — SCI_014 (Polymers Alkali Treatment) and INBAR safety protocols (SCI_013).*

> **Caution**
> **Scientific Alert (2025):** Beyond chemical baths, the processing dust itself requires biosafety. A peer-reviewed study published in 2025 evidenced the **carcinogenic toxicity of bamboo shavings in Acre** when inhaled without PPE or processed at scale without exhaust. This destroys the naive myth that "since it's a natural plant, any bamboo dust and residue is harmless."

## 3. Ecological Alternatives

The first step for the biobuilder is to use efficient treatments that do not poison the environment, supported by the most recent tests, theses, and articles (such as those in the MQTF scientific collection):

1. **Forest Curing:** Leave the cut bamboo standing, resting on a stone (no contact with the ground) for 30 days. The leaves continue transpiring sap, while the starch decants and ferments at the base, repelling borers.
2. **Alkalization Protocol with Ash Milk:** A direct evolution of research from the IFB kiln project. With the scientific deepening of the MQTF collection, it was found that alkaline baths (using the ashes from carbonization itself) neutralize sugars and sterilize bamboo. It is a clean, closed-loop technique.
3. **Extract Immersion:** The use of tannins, tar, and especially **Pyroligneous Extract** (a product from the Biorefinery) offers natural antifungal action and replaces isolated chemicals.
4. **Heat Treatment:** Steaming in a clean autoclave or direct fire, caramelizing the starch and carbonizing the outer layer to seal the pores.

*Note to Mentors:* When guiding a student, deconstruct the discourse that "Borax is 100% natural and harmless." It is better than Arsenic, but it is far from being scalable in a responsible manner. The definitive waterproofing solution is physical (thermal) and clean chemistry (such as Vegetable PU which seals the treated piece).
